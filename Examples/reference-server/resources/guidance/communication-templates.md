# Incident Communication Templates

## Internal Status Updates

### Critical Incident - Initial Alert
```
🚨 CRITICAL INCIDENT - [INCIDENT_ID]

**Status**: INVESTIGATING
**Impact**: [Describe user impact]
**Started**: [TIME]
**Next Update**: [TIME]

**Actions Taken**:
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]

**War Room**: [Bridge/Channel Info]
**Incident Commander**: [Name]
```

### Progress Update
```
📊 UPDATE - [INCIDENT_ID]

**Status**: [CURRENT_STATUS]
**Progress**: [What's been done since last update]
**Current Actions**: [What's happening now]
**Next Update**: [TIME]

**Timeline**:
- [TIME]: [Event]
- [TIME]: [Event]

**Needs**: [Any help/resources needed]
```

### Resolution Notice
```
✅ RESOLVED - [INCIDENT_ID]

**Resolution**: [Brief description of fix]
**Root Cause**: [Brief cause summary]

**Impact**: [Final impact assessment]

**Next Steps**:
- Monitoring for [DURATION]

```

## Customer Communications

### Service Disruption Notice
```
Subject: [Service Name] Service Disruption - We're Working on a Fix

We're currently experiencing issues with [Service Name] that began at [TIME].

**Current Impact:**
[Describe what users are experiencing]

**What We're Doing:**
Our team is actively working to resolve this issue. We have identified [brief description if known] and are implementing a fix.

**Next Update:**
We'll provide another update within [TIMEFRAME] or sooner if resolved.

We apologize for the inconvenience and appreciate your patience.

[Company] Support Team
```

### Resolution Announcement
```
Subject: [Service Name] Issue Resolved - Service Restored

The service disruption affecting [Service Name] has been resolved as of [TIME].

**What Happened:**
[Brief, non-technical explanation]

**Resolution:**
[Brief description of fix applied]

**Prevention:**
We're implementing additional measures to prevent similar issues in the future.

**Questions?**
If you continue to experience issues, please contact support at [CONTACT].

Thank you for your patience during this incident.

[Company] Support Team
```

## Key Guidelines

### Voice and Tone
- **Be direct and honest** - Don't sugar-coat or deflect
- **Use simple language** - Avoid technical jargon for customer comms
- **Take ownership** - Use "we" not "the system" or "the platform"
- **Show empathy** - Acknowledge the frustration this causes

### Timing
- **Acknowledge quickly** - Better to say "we're investigating" than stay silent
- **Update regularly** - Even if there's no progress to report
- **Set expectations** - When will you update next?
- **Follow through** - Always deliver on communication commitments

### Information Management
- **Stick to facts** - Don't speculate or guess
- **Be consistent** - Ensure all channels have same information
- **Escalate appropriately** - Know when to involve legal/PR/executives
- **Document everything** - Save communications for post-mortem review

---
*These templates should be customized for your organization's voice, legal requirements, and communication policies.*
